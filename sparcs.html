Star-Planet Activity Research CubeSat

Mission Goal

A team of graduate researchers and ASU staff are working to develop a small space telescope with the mission of monitoring the flares and sunspot activity of M-type stars.

My Part in This

Initially, I started out by designing mounting jigs to test different optics in the CubeSat. The components I modeled and drafted were machined professionally by the ASU Machine and Electrical Shop.

I then started to assist more in the cleaning, prep, and assembly of different parts used for testing - all while maintaining ISO 5 cleanroom protocols.

For some extended thermal tests, I helped out by taking two 8hr late-night shifts where I monitored an LN2 cooling setup and adjusted when necessary.

I also assisted in extensive disassembly, cleaning, and reassembly of a vacuum chamber while in the cleanroom in order to reduce contaminants.

Helping out here has given me a substantial amount of experience with vacuum chambers, liquid nitrogen cooling, CAD design and drafting, and ISO-5 cleanrooms. I've thoroughly enjoyed my time here and plan to continue as long as my studies permit me.
